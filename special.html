the land of devine giants

There is magic in the air when you find yourself on the world's second highest plateau. Welcome to deosai national park in Skardu Pakistan, the land of devine giants.


Deosai is a combination of two words ‘Deo’ (giant) and ‘Sai’ (shadow). For a very long time, it was considered that this place is haunted by giants, thus the name ‘The Land of the Giants’ came into being.

lake of fairies

The Lake Saiful Muluk is named after a legendary prince. A fairy tale called Saif-ul-Muluk, tells the story of the prince of Egypt named Prince Saiful Malook who fell in love with a fairy princess named Princess Badri-ul-Jamala at the lake.


The prince searched six years for princess and discovered she was trapped by by Safaid Deyo (white giant) for the past 10 years. The white giant was also in love with the fairy.

Legend has it that the Ansoo Lake, a few miles away from Saif-ul-Malook, was created out of the tears of the white giant when he found out that the fairy was gone.

Heaven on earth

Hunza valley is Described as a permanently happy land and "heaven on earth" and. The people of hunza valley are isolated from the world with a life expectancy of over 120 years.


Water Flowing from the melted glaciers in Hunza is h20 in it's most pure and natural form. The water is considered "The Fountain of youth" and is believed to be "magic water"

Eight wonder of the world

The great karakoram highway is the highest paved international road in the world. It is considered as one of the most incredible engineering projects of the century due to the altitude and hard conditions in which it was built and took over 27 years to build with lives of nearly 1000 workers


What was once an important ”Silk Road” used to link the Indian sub-continent with China, is Considered to be one of the highest roads in the world at an elevation of 4,693 metres (15,397 ft) above the sea level.
