Welcome To Historic Life Of Pakistan


Empire of great dynasties

The city of Lahore is the capital of the Punjab province and was part of the Mughal, Sikh and British Empire. While the city existed for over a thousand of years, it was after 1947, Pakistan gained it's independence and the punjab province was partioned to Pakistan.

Several of the monuments built in and around Lahore under the Mughals, the Sikhs, and the British have survived. They are reminders of the vital role the city has played throughout the history of the Indian subcontinent.

Mughal Dynasty

The Mughals were patrons of architecture, who left behind a rich culture that lived on after their influence had ended. The monuments in Lahore are reminders of the roles various rulers played in the city's history.

The Mughal rulers built their forts, palaces, gardens and mosques for queens, emperors and other noblemen in and around Lahore to project power, authority and impress their subjects through their display of wealth.

The city of lahore is the second largest city and considered the heart of Pakistan



Sikh Dynasty

In the interval between the falling of Mughal Empire and the rule by the British Empire, Lahore served as the capital of the Sikh Empire of Mahraja Rangit Singh (1780-1839) After taking over lahore, Ranjit took the title of Maharaja of the punjab.

Ranjit Singh formed his independent Sikh empire in Lahore as its capital.

Dynasty of Pakistan


Muhammad Ali Jinnah founded pakistan on August 14, 1947. Also referred as Quaid-i-Azam ("Great Leader") and Baba-i-Qaum, ("Father of the Nation"), Jinnah believed that Muslims of the Indian subcontinent should have their own state.

Partition was followed by with mass migration of muslims, hindus and sikhs to resettle within the new borders. With the arrival of indian muslims and departure of Pakistan's Hindus and sikhs, pakistan finally gained it's independence
