The Mughals were patrons of architecture, who left behind a rich culture that lived on after their influence had ended. The monuments in Lahore are reminders of the roles various rulers played in the city's history.

The Mughal rulers built their forts, palaces, gardens and mosques for queens, emperors and other noblemen in and around Lahore to project power, authority and impress their subjects through their display of wealth.
